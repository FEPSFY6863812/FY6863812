Introduction

          The automobile industry has undergone major technological milestones ever since the debut of the first automobile 
  powered by internal combustion that set place over one hundred years ago; the ICE (internal combustion engine) has remained 
  the dominant powertrain systems for automobiles for the entire 20th century, but this has started to change as we enter the 21st. 
  With decarbonization being one of the top priorities in modern transportation, car manufacturers around the world have started to 
  take action, constantly in the process of researching and developing a greener, more environment-friendly alternative to ICE when 
  it comes to vehicle powertrain systems. As of 2022, electricity is believed to be the main alternative, with “2.7 million public 
  charging points worldwide, more than 900 000 of which were installed in 2022, about a 55% increase on 2021 stock” (IEA, 2023). 
  While electric-powered vehicles are well integrated into the real world, there is another source of power which has not been the 
  main topic of discussion, yet it could be a major breakthrough in achieving zero-carbon emission in modern vehicles: Hydrogen. 
  Elemental hydrogen is the most abundant element in the universe; it has the “highest energy content of any common fuel by weight” 
  and can be produced organically from any sort of biomass (U.S. Energy Information Administration, 2022). At face value, hydrogen 
  seems to be the catalyst for many car manufacturers to achieve net zero carbon emissions in their vehicles, yet the number of
  hydrogen-powered cars registered worldwide is greatly underwhelming when being compared with the other two power sources; only 
  70,200 FCEV (fuel cell electric vehicles) are registered worldwide, compared to the 26 million PHEV (Plug-in electric vehicle) 
  that are on the road worldwide in the same year (Chu and Cui, 2023; IEA pdf*, 2023). Therefore, this paper aims to investigate 
  the eligibility of hydrogen as a fuel source for modern automobiles by evaluating the advantages and disadvantages of hydrogen 
  fuel in a real world scenario, followed by statistical data to further develop each argument.
